The documents your enterprise runs on weren't built for AI

Your employees are already using AI to query internal documents, but those documents were designed to be read by humans, not machines.

Section 1

Why data quality matters for reliable answers

Enterprise AI is only as trustworthy as the documentation behind it. When employees query internal systems for policy guidance, compliance requirements, technical procedures, or contract terms, the accuracy of those answers is reliant on how well the machine can read a document that's built to be comprehended visually.

Most organizations won't discover this problem until an AI returns a consequential wrong answer. Your AI system has no way to flag when it's working from broken content; it retrieves what it finds and returns a confident answer regardless.

HIGH RISK

Compliance and Legal

Dense tables, footnotes, multi-column layouts are standard formatting.

A wrong answer carries legal and regulatory risk.

HIGH RISK

HR Handbooks and Policy

Long, version-controlled documents queried constantly by employees.

Policies get misquoted at scale. Employees act on wrong information.

HIGH RISK

Technical Specifications

Numbered procedures, tables, and diagrams with callouts throughout.

Steps separate from context. In safety-critical environments, this is dangerous.

Section 2

What the Machine Actually Reads

When an AI processes a PDF it does not read the visual page. It reads the tag tree, a hidden structural layer embedded in the file that describes content in sequence.

Unstructured PDF

Adobe SOC 2 Type 2 report, two-column layout, no tag structure:

"Service Organization's Responsibilities Adobe is responsible Our responsibility is to express an opinion Service Auditor's Responsibilities for its service commitments and system requirements..."

The AI receives both sections interleaved. It cannot tell which sentence belongs to which heading, so any answer about Adobe's audit responsibilities will be wrong.

Structured PDF

Same document, correct reading order and tags:

"Service Organization's Responsibilities. Adobe is responsible for its service commitments and system requirements and for designing, implementing, and operating effective controls... [section complete] Service Auditor's Responsibilities. Our responsibility is to express an opinion on the description and on the suitability of the design..."

Same PDF. Same visual layout. Each section is complete and attributed correctly.

Section 4

Making PDFs AI-Ready: Step by Step

1
Start in the Source Document
⌄
  • Use Heading 1, 2, 3 styles in the Styles panel, never bold or resize text to fake a heading
  • Keep layouts single-column where possible
  • Build tables with clear column headers and simple row structure
  • Add alt text to any image that carries meaning
Watch: Prepping Source Files for Accessibility
2
Run the Make Accessible Guided Action Acrobat Pro Required
⌄

In Acrobat Pro: All Tools > Use Guided Actions > Make Accessible. This automated action handles the foundational work before you do any manual review.

  • Adds document title and description
  • Runs OCR on scanned pages
  • Auto-tags the document structure
  • Sets the reading language
  • Prompts for alt text on images
  • Runs the Accessibility Checker at the end and flags what still needs attention
Why this matters for ingestion

Without tags: An untagged PDF is a flat string of text to any AI. There is no hierarchy, no section boundaries, and no way to know where one topic ends and another begins. Chunks get formed arbitrarily and lose their context.

With tags: A tagged document gives the AI a map. Chunks can form at heading boundaries and paragraph breaks, which are meaningful units. Every retrieved piece of content still knows what section it belongs to.

Watch: Let Acrobat help you make accessible PDFs
3
Review and Fix Reading Order Acrobat Pro Required
⌄

In Acrobat Pro: All Tools > Prepare for Accessibility > Fix Reading Order. The tool overlays numbered boxes on the page. Those numbers are the exact sequence the machine reads your content in.

  • Check that numbers follow a logical path through the document
  • For multi-column layouts: confirm it reads all of column 1 before moving to column 2
  • Reorder boxes by dragging to correct any sequence errors
  • Tag headers, footers, and decorative elements as Background/Artifact to exclude them from the reading sequence
Why this matters for ingestion

Reading order is what determines how chunks form. If the tag tree reads across two columns line by line, a chunk might start mid-sentence in column one and finish mid-sentence in column two. That chunk is meaningless when retrieved and will produce a confused or wrong answer. Correct reading order means chunks form along the natural flow of the document, so retrieved content is always coherent and in context.

4
Verify and Fix Tags Acrobat Pro Required
⌄

Open the Tags panel: View > Show/Hide > Navigation Panes > Tags.

  • Confirm heading hierarchy: H1 for title, H2 for sections, H3 for subsections. No skipped levels.
  • Verify tables have TH (header) and TD (data) cells correctly assigned. Right-click a tag > Table Cell Properties to fix.
  • Ensure decorative images are marked as artifacts, not figures
  • Add or correct alt text: right-click a Figure tag > Edit Alternate Text
  • Reorder tags by dragging within the panel if reading sequence is still wrong

Section 4

Solving This at Scale with Acrobat Analyzer

The steps in this guide fix individual PDFs. Acrobat Analyzer handles the extraction problem at enterprise scale, reliably, even from imperfect documents.

Define Once

Write what you need to extract in plain language. Analyzer applies it across thousands of documents automatically.

Cited Results

Every extracted value links back to the exact source text. No answer without a verifiable reference.

Structured Output

Export to CSV or connect directly to CRM, ERP, and compliance systems via API.

Where this matters most

Finance

Contract Review

Extract payment terms and performance obligations from thousands of contracts, consistently, without manual review.

Compliance

Vendor Agreements

Track SLA obligations across hundreds of vendor agreements and flag deviations before they become incidents.

HR

Policy Consistency

Ensure policy documents are interpreted consistently at scale and surface conflicts across versions before they reach employees.

Procurement

Rate Benchmarking

Benchmark vendor rates across contracts with different formats and structures, without normalizing every document first.
